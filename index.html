Taylor Alison Swift

In the tapestry of melodies, Taylor Swift stitches the heart's secrets into every verse she weaves.

Taylor Swift has established herself as one of the most successful and prolific artists of her generation.Over her twenty-year career, she has never ceased to amaze audiences all over the world with her extraordinary talent as a singer-songwriter.
I've put together a timeline of Taylor Swift's career highlights to honor not only a talented musician but also a cultural icon that still inspires and influences a great number of musicians and fans.

Here's a time line of Taylor Swift's life:

  • 1989 : Born on December 13, in West Reading, Pennsylvania to Andrea and Scott Swift
  • 2001 :Learned to play guitar and started penning some songs
  • 2003 : The Swift family relocated to Hendersonville, Tennesse. Taylor's proximity to Nashville, the epicenter of country music, was the driving force behind the relocation.
  • 2005 : She was signed by Scott Borchetta's recently founded Big Machine Records. According to the terms of the deal, Swift would put out six albums through the label.
  • 2006 : Released her first single, ‘Tim McGraw’.
  • 2006 : "Taylor Swift," Swift's debut studio album, was released on 24th October.
  • 2007 : Released the EP 'The Taylor Swift Holiday Collection' on 14th October.
  • 2007 : The "Taylor Swift" album went on to sell over a million copies in the US, earning the singer a platinum certification and the Country Music Association's (CMA) November 2007 Horizon Award for best new artist.
  • 2008 : Released the EP 'Beautiful Eyes' on 15th July.
  • 2008 : Swift's second album "Fearless," which was released on November 11, sold more than half a million copies in its first week of release, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 list. The record, which showed off a sophisticated pop sensibility, courted the mainstream pop audience without losing sight of the singer's countryside roots.
  • 2009 : Embarked on her first ever World Tour.
  • 2009 : The Academy of Country Music named "Fearless" album of the year in April, and in September, Swift wins the MTV Video Music Awards (VMAs) for best female video with "You Belong with Me."
    However rapper Kanye West's infamous interruption during her acceptance speech, during which he protested that Beyoncé should have won, marred the momentous occasion. A standing ovation was given to both performers when Beyoncé brought Taylor on stage to wrap off her remarks later in the show while winning the video of the year award.
  • 2010 : Taylor Swift begins the year with an impressive showing at the Grammy Awards, collecting 4 honors, including the best country song, best country album, and album of the year on 31st January, becoming the youngest artist to do so at the time.
  • 2010 : Released her 3rd album entitled “Speak Now” on October 25. This was the first album of hers where she was the only songwriter.
  • 2011 : Received the Entertainer of the Year Award from the Country Music Association.
    And also won Artist of the Year and Favourite Country Album at the American Music Awards on 20th November.
  • 2012 : In just 50 minutes after its release on August 14, her song "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together" became the number one song on the iTunes singles chart, setting a Guiness World Record for her.
  • 2012 : "Red," which was released on October 22, sold 1.2 million copies in the US during its first week of sales, the most in ten years. Furthermore, the lead single from it, "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together," gave Swift her first number-one success on the pop singles chart of Billboard.
  • 2013 : Swift began to tour for her 4th album "Red" in March
  • 2013 : On 25th August, her single, "I Knew You Were Trouble" won the Best Female Video at the MTV Video Music Awards.
  • 2014 : In March Tyalor moved to New York City.
  • 2014 : Titled after her birth year and reportedly inspired by the music of that era, “1989”, released on 27th October, was what Taylor would call her first “official pop album”.
  • 2014 : In November, Swift took all of her songs down from Spotify since the free service reduced the amount of royalties that artists got.
  • 2015 : On 5th May, she began touring - "1989 World Tour"
  • 2015 : In June, Taylor filed a complaint with Apple Music alleging that during the free trial period, artists were not paid royalties. She made a threat to take "1989" off of their system. Very shortly, Apple reversed course and said that during consumers' free trials, artists would get royalties.
  • 2016 : Swifts album ‘1989’ won Album of the Year, Best Pop Vocal Album and Best Music Video (Bad Blood) at the Grammy Awards.
  • 2016 : During the release of his song "Famous" in April 2016, Kanye West made reference to Swift in the line, "I made that bitch famous." Swift took issue with West, claiming that she never gave her okay for the lyric. However, West countered that she did, and his ex-wife Kim Kardashian shared video clips of the two of them having a cordial phone conversation about the song which was later proved to be edited. Online "cancel" movements centered around Swift as a result of the scandal.
  • 2017 : Swift re-released her entire discography on Google Play, Spotify, and Amazon Music in June.
  • 2017 : David Mueller, a former radio host for KYGO-FM, sued Swift in August but was successfully countersued by Swift, who was seeking damages for job loss. She told Mueller's superiors that he had touched her inappropriately during an occasion four years prior.
  • 2017 : Swift released her 6th studio album - "Reputation" on November 10th. At the time she coined the phrase - "There will be no explanation, there will just be reputation" and went along with it.
  • 2018 : On May 8th, Swfit embarked on her Reputation Stadium Tour which became the highest-grossing North American concert tour in history and grossed $345.7 million worldwide.
  • 2018 : Following the expiration of her contract with Big Machine Records, Taylor Swift entered into a new agreement with distributor Universal Music Group. Big Machine refused to provide Swift the masters for her first six albums, despite her request.
  • 2019 : Big Machine Records was bought by Scooter Braun. The masters of Taylor Swift's first six albums were included in the auction. Big Machine Records' Scott Borchetta claimed that Swift had turned down a chance to buy the masters; Swift denied this, and the two got into an intense disagreement.
  • 2019 : Released on August 23, the album was described by the artist as a “love letter to love itself”, with same-titled song being a highly upbeat record.
  • 2020 : Due to the coronavirus outbreak, Taylor Swift's planned "Lover Fest" tour of Europe and America had to be postponed.
  • 2020 : "Folklore," which was released on July 24 with minimal announcement, was praised for its reflection and was a change from the pop-inspired work she had previously done.
  • 2020 : Swift's first six albums' masters were sold by Scooter Braun to Shamrock Holdings, the Disney family investment firm. Braun would continue to get payment for the material even after the sale. Taylor Swift expressed her disapproval of this arrangement and reiterated her plan to rerecord her first six albums under the label of Taylor's Versions.
  • 2020 : The sister record, “Evermore”, was released later on December 11 2020
  • 2021 : The single ‘Love Story (Taylor’s Version)’ was released - her first re-recorded song.
  • 2021 : The album ‘Fearless (Taylor’s Version)’ was released on April 9. This re-recording of her 2008 album reached number 1 in the UK and US charts
  • 2021 : Swift received a Global Icon award at the Brits on May 11.
  • 2021 : On November 12, The album ‘Red (Taylor’s Version)’ was released. This re-recording of her 2012 album reached number 1 in the UK and US charts. And the All Too Well (10 minute version) (Taylor's Version) became the first 10 minute song to ever top the Billboard's Hot 100 list.
  • 2022 : On October 21, the candid album "Midnights" was published, featuring a synth-pop vibe. It is, in her words, "the story of 13 sleepless nights scattered throughout my life."
    This album broke many records.
    • Became the most streamed album in a day and in a week on Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music.
    • Broke multiple Guiness World Records.
    • Taylor became the first person to occupy the entire Billboard's Hot 100
  • 2023 : Taylor Swift's sixth concert tour overall and her first since 2018 began with the Eras Tour in March 2023. With 151 dates spread across five continents, the Eras Tour is her most extensive tour to date, as she puts it: "a journey through all of her musical eras." This is also the longest duration her concert has ever been - 3.5 hours
  • 2023 : Swift won the 2023 MTV Video Music Awards, winning nine of the most coveted categories, including Song of the Year, Best Pop Video, and, of course, Video of the Year for her song "Anti-Hero." She is the most honored individual in this category having won this coveted award four times.
  • 2023 : According to a study by Bloomberg, as of October 2023, Taylor Swift’s entire net worth was estimated at roughly $1.1 billion. She is therefore one of the few musicians to achieve such a high profile purely through their music and her tours.
  • 2024 : At the 2024 Grammy Awards, Taylor Swift creates music history by becoming the first artist to win album of the year four times.
    While accepting her award for the Pop Vocal Album of the Year, she announced her 11th album - 'The Tortured Poets Department'.
  • 2024 : On April 19th, Taylor released 'The Tortured Poets Department' and just 2 hours after it's release, she announced that it was a double album, making the album to have a total of 31 tracks.
    With this album, she broke her own record of the Spotify's most streamed album in a day with 313 million streams.
